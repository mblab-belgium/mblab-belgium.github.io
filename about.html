Who am i?


My name is Mathias Boeckx, the "MB" from the lab.

I come from the great country of belgium and on this page

you can read a little about my education, hobbys and interest.



Education


Bachelor degree

Right now, I'm studying for a bachelor's degree in Cloud & Cybersecurity at Thomas More in Geel.

This course teaches you how to set up your own cloud infrastructure, automate it, and secure it properly.

I chose this path because I think security is often overlooked or only considered afterwards.

With this degree, I’m making sure that’s not the case for me, and that I’ve got the knowledge

to build security in right from the start in all my future projects and jobs.


Associate degree

Before starting my bachelor’s, I completed an associate degree in IoT (Internet of Things).

This course mainly focused on collecting and processing data, and controlling actuators with microcontrollers.

What I found most interesting was the mix of hardware and software.

I really enjoy working with electronics, and when you connect that with software,

you can bring things to life pretty quickly.

I finished this degree with the highest distinction, something I’m definitely proud of.



Hobbys


Climbing

My main hobby is climbing, it's something I really enjoy and try to do as often as I can.

What I love about climbing is that it's not just a physical puzzle, but also a mental challenge.

You can’t climb without feeling fear. You have to learn how to manage that fear and keep going,

while making the right judgment about when things are worth doing and when it’s getting too dangerous.


Surfing

When I’ve had a tough or exhausting period, I try to go surfing.

With surfing, the mental challenge is a bit less intense and the daily rhythm

slows down, which gives me more time to really relax.



Interests


Traveling

Like most people my age, I enjoy traveling. I love discovering new places, trying different foods, and experiencing new cultures.

Most of the countries I’ve visited are in Europe, except for Tanzania. I went there during middle school for a volunteer project.


Books

Another thing I really enjoy in my free time is reading.

One of the first books I read was 'Dune'. People who know it will understand it’s a heavy book to start with,

but luckily, the story grabbed me right away and pulled me in.

After that, I started looking for other 'space operas' and ended up reading 'The Three-Body Problem' and 'Hyperion'.

I found all three of these series absolutely amazing, and I’d recommend them to anyone interested in the genre.


Since I started spending quite a bit of time reading, I thought it might be a good idea to also pick up something more educational now and then.

That’s why I also started reading non-fiction books like 'Sapiens' from Yuval Noah Harari.
